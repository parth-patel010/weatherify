# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

As contributors and maintainers of Weatherify, we pledge to make participation in our project and our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

This Code of Conduct applies both within project spaces and in public spaces when an individual is representing the project (e.g., on social media, in issue trackers, and in mailing lists).

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:
- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism
- Focusing on what is best for the community
- Showing empathy toward other community members

Examples of unacceptable behavior by participants include:
- The use of sexualized language or imagery and unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Trolling, insulting/derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others’ private information, such as a physical or electronic address, without explicit permission
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing this Code of Conduct. Maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies both within project spaces and in public spaces when an individual is representing the project. Examples of representing the project include using an official project e-mail address, posting via an official social media account, or acting as an appointed representative at an online or offline event.

## Reporting Guidelines

If you are subject to or witness a behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, please report it as soon as possible.

Preferred reporting options:
- Create a private issue in this repository titled "Code of Conduct incident" (maintainers will handle it confidentially), or
- Contact the maintainers directly:
  - GitHub: @ashujsrfox
  - Maintainers: (replace with preferred contact email(s)): maintainer@example.com

When reporting, please include:
- A clear description of the behavior that violated the Code of Conduct
- The names (GitHub handles) of those involved
- Where and when the incident occurred (links to issues/PRs/threads if applicable)
- Any evidence (screenshots, logs, etc.)
- Whether you would like a private response

All reports will be handled with discretion and will not be shared publicly except as necessary to investigate and address the issue or as required by law.

## Enforcement Process

1. Once a report is received, maintainers will review it promptly and may request additional information from the reporter.
2. Maintainers will determine appropriate actions, which may include:
   - A private conversation with the offending party
   - Temporary or permanent exclusion from the project (e.g., blocking, restricting access)
   - Public or private apology
   - Referral to relevant authorities in severe cases
3. The outcome of an investigation will be communicated to the reporter whenever possible, respecting privacy and legal constraints.
